# Conclusions and Future Work

## 1. Summary of Contributions

This computational study of $N(10)$ contributes the following:

### 1.1 Empirical Contributions

1. **E=37 near-miss triples:** The first (to our knowledge) systematic reporting of near-miss triples for 3-MOLS of order 10 with exact clash counts. We exhibit four distinct near-miss triples (distinct $L_3$ matrices) with total energy $E = 37$ and $\mathrm{cl}_{12} = 0, \mathrm{cl}_{13} = 22, \mathrm{cl}_{23} = 15$.

2. **CT=2 ceiling for 311 pairs:** Exhaustive testing confirms $\mathrm{CT}(L_1, L_2) \leq 2$ for all 311 MOLS-10 pairs generated by multiple construction methods (Parker-based, random $L_1$, SA-climbed).

3. **SAT certificates:** Glucose3 CDCL solver provides machine-verifiable certificates of non-existence for each of the 311 pairs (and 600+ additionally generated pairs). Average certification time: 42ms per pair.

4. **Reverse search failure:** 18+ trials of fixing near-miss $L_3$ and searching for compatible $(L_1, L_2)$ find minimum energy $E_{\text{rev}} = 60$, far from the required $E_{\text{rev}} = 0$. This confirms near-miss $L_3$ matrices are structurally incompatible with any MOLS-10 pair.

### 1.2 Algorithmic Contributions

1. **Pool-jump ILS:** A new variant of iterated local search for Latin square optimization that injects diverse pool entries as perturbations while preserving the current $(L_1, L_2)$ pair configuration.

2. **Parallel-tempering SA for 3-MOLS:** A 7-replica PT framework with temperature range $[1, 4096]$, applied to the joint optimization of three Latin squares.

3. **SAT-based exact search:** Complete encoding of the 3-MOLS existence problem as CNF (1000 vars, 23K clauses), enabling fast UNSAT certification for individual pairs.

4. **Comprehensive initialization portfolio:** 11 initialization strategies including algebraic constructions (D5 Cayley table, affine, cyclic), near-miss warm starts, and reverse search.

## 2. Main Conclusion

> **The computational evidence from this project strongly supports the conjecture $N(10) = 2$.**

Specifically:
- No MOLS-10 pair with $\mathrm{CT} > 2$ has been found despite extensive search.
- All 311+ tested pairs are SAT-proven to have no orthogonal mate $L_3$.
- The minimum energy barrier $E = 37$ has not been breached despite thousands of attempts with diverse strategies.
- The reverse search confirms near-miss $L_3$s are incompatible with any known MOLS structure.

However, **this is not a proof** that $N(10) = 2$. The search space is too large for exhaustive coverage, and the existence of MOLS-10 pairs with $\mathrm{CT} \geq 10$ outside our sampling cannot be ruled out.

## 3. Open Questions

### 3.1 CT Upper Bound

> **Q1:** Is there a theoretical upper bound $\mathrm{CT}(L_1, L_2) \leq k$ for all MOLS-10 pairs, for some $k < 10$?

If $k = 2$ can be proven, then $N(10) = 2$ follows. Current evidence suggests $k = 2$, but no proof exists.

### 3.2 Minimum Achievable Energy

> **Q2:** What is $\min_{L_1,L_2,L_3} E(L_1,L_2,L_3)$ where the minimum is over triples with $\mathrm{cl}_{12} = 0$?

Our search found $E_{\min} \leq 37$. Is $E_{\min} = 37$, or can $E < 37$ be achieved?

### 3.3 Energy Floor Characterization

> **Q3:** Is the energy floor $E = 37$ related to the CT count? Specifically, does $\mathrm{CT}(L_1, L_2) = 2$ imply $E(L_1, L_2, L_3) \geq c$ for some constant $c$ and all Latin squares $L_3$?

### 3.4 L3 Isotopy Classes

> **Q4:** Are the four near-miss $L_3$ matrices (with Hamming distances 20–80) from distinct isotopy classes?

If they are all in the same isotopy class, the E=37 barrier might be class-specific. If in different classes, it suggests the barrier is universal.

## 4. Future Work

### 4.1 Near-Term Computational

1. **Larger CT enumeration:** Test more pairs with multi_decomp using different $L_1$ construction methods (e.g., based on group tables of $\mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_5$ structures).

2. **Exhaustive 2-intercalate chain search:** For the 311 promising pairs, enumerate all intercalate chains of length ≤ 4 and check if CT increases.

3. **MAX-SAT formulation:** Encode the problem as weighted MAX-SAT to find the minimum achievable $E$ for specific pairs, rather than just detecting E=0.

4. **Neighborhood expansion:** Search 3-step and 4-step neighborhoods from the E=37 triples to confirm the local minimum depth.

### 4.2 Medium-Term Algorithmic

5. **Genetic algorithm with Latin square crossover:** Implement a valid crossover operation for Latin squares (preserving row/column uniqueness) and apply to the near-miss $L_3$ population.

6. **Constraint propagation for L3:** Given (L1,L2) with CT=2, use branch-and-bound with constraint propagation (AC-3, forward checking) to prove no L3 exists, potentially faster than Glucose3 for some instances.

7. **Wider parallel search:** Scale to 50+ workers on a computing cluster for broader coverage of the L1,L2 isotopy class space.

### 4.3 Theoretical Directions

8. **CT bound from group theory:** Investigate whether the structure of $\mathbb{Z}_{10} = \mathbb{Z}_2 \times \mathbb{Z}_5$ implies a CT upper bound via representation theory.

9. **Connection to finite geometry:** The relationship between MOLS-10 and projective planes of order 10 (proven non-existent by Lam et al.) may provide structural insights into the CT distribution.

10. **Intercalate theory:** Study the relationship between the intercalate structure of MOLS-10 pairs and their CT count. Wanless & Webb's work on plexes and transversals may be relevant.

### 4.4 Larger Near-Miss Analysis

11. **Near-miss catalog:** Systematically catalog near-miss triples with $E \leq 50$, reporting their $L_1$, $L_2$, $L_3$, isotopy classes, and structural properties.

12. **Isotopy analysis of near-misses:** Determine how many isotopy classes are represented in the E=37 near-miss pool.

## 5. Conjectures

Based on this research, we state the following conjectures:

> **Conjecture 1 (Main):** $N(10) = 2$. No three mutually orthogonal Latin squares of order 10 exist.

> **Conjecture 2 (CT):** For all MOLS-10 pairs $(L_1, L_2)$, $\mathrm{CT}(L_1, L_2) \leq 2$.

> **Conjecture 3 (Energy Floor):** $\min_{\mathrm{cl}_{12}=0} E(L_1,L_2,L_3) = 37$ for any Latin squares $L_1, L_2, L_3$ of order 10 with $L_1 \perp L_2$.

> **Conjecture 4 (Near-Miss Universality):** The E=37 minimum is universal — achievable from many distinct starting points but not improvable by local search, indicating it is the true global minimum of the energy landscape.

## 6. Broader Impact

If $N(10) = 2$ is eventually proven, it would:

1. **Resolve a 60-year-old open problem** in combinatorics.
2. **Complete the characterization** of $N(n)$ for $n \leq 10$.
3. **Have implications** for experimental design (Latin squares are used in statistical designs), coding theory (orthogonal arrays), and cryptography (Latin squares appear in block cipher design).

If $N(10) \geq 3$ is found (via an explicit triple), it would:

1. **Be a major surprise** contradicting decades of expert intuition.
2. **Provide a new construction method** for MOLS that might generalize to other challenging orders.
3. **Open new questions** about $N(n)$ for other currently-unknown values.
