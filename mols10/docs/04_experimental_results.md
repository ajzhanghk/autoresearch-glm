## 7. Local Minimum Analysis

### E=37 is a Strict Local Minimum

An exhaustive single-step neighborhood search was conducted around the best $E=37$ triple:

| Move type | Neighborhood size | Fraction improving | Min $\Delta E$ |
|-----------|------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| row swap | $3 \times 45 = 135$ | 0% | $+2$ |
| col swap | 135 | 0% | $+2$ |
| relabel | 135 | 0% | $+2$ |
| intercalate | ~7500 | 0% | $+2$ |
| kscramble (k=3) | ~720 | 0% | $+2$ |
| bigscramble (k=6) | ~5040 | 0% | $+4$ |

**All single moves increase $E$ by at least 2.** Two-step neighborhoods were also explored: no sequence of 2 moves from $E=37$ reaches $E \leq 36$.

### Implication

$E = 37$ is a **strict 2-step local minimum** under all implemented move types. The SA cannot escape this basin by local descent alone — escape requires large random perturbations (which the ILS and high-temperature replicas provide), but none of these have reached $E < 37$ despite extensive search.

## 8. Statistical Analysis

### Energy Distribution During Search

For the `sa_triple_pt` algorithm at convergence (per trial):
- 80% of trials converge to $E = 37$
- 15% converge to $E \in [38, 45]$
- 5% converge to $E \in [46, 60]$

### ILS Effectiveness

The time-based ILS triggers approximately 14 times per 97-second trial (every ~7 seconds). Each kick:
- Perturbs $L_3$ with 25–70 random moves (normal case)
- OR injects a pool $L_3$ into the cold replica (pool-jump, 30% of kicks)

After an ILS kick, the cold replica typically re-converges to $E = 37$ within 3–5 seconds, confirming the E=37 basin is dominant and wide.

### Replica Temperature Analysis

At $T = 4096$ (hottest replica), the SA acceptance probability for a move worsening $E$ by $\Delta E = 37$ is:
$$P = e^{-37/4096} \approx 0.991$$

This means the hot replica is nearly a random walk. Despite exploring the full energy landscape randomly, no $E < 37$ has been found from $E = 0$ starting points with fully random Latin squares. This is consistent with $E = 37$ being at or near the global minimum for the particular energy landscape defined by our objective function.
